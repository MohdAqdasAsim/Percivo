# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We, as contributors, maintainers, and community members of **Percivo**, are committed to making participation in this project a respectful, inclusive, and harassment-free experience for everyone.

We welcome contributors of all backgrounds, identities, and levels of experience. This includes — but is not limited to — differences in age, body size, disability, ethnicity, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socioeconomic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, and sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, and healthy community.

## Our Standards

### Expected Behavior

All community members are expected to:

- **Be respectful and considerate** in all interactions — in issues, pull requests, discussions, and any other project spaces
- **Use welcoming and inclusive language** — prefer language that invites participation over language that excludes
- **Be constructive** when giving feedback — focus on the work, not the person; acknowledge effort before pointing out problems
- **Accept feedback gracefully** — disagreement is normal; personal attacks are not
- **Acknowledge and credit** the contributions of others
- **Be patient and supportive** with newer contributors or those still learning
- **Prioritize the well-being of the community** — especially when navigating conflict or disagreement

### Unacceptable Behavior

The following behaviors are considered unacceptable within this project and its community spaces:

- **Harassment** of any kind, including offensive comments, personal attacks, or unwelcome attention
- **Discrimination** based on any personal characteristic listed in our pledge above
- **Trolling** — deliberately inflammatory, insulting, or disruptive comments
- **Doxxing** — publishing private information about others without explicit consent
- **Sexual or violent content** in any form, including sexualized language, imagery, or unwelcome advances
- **Sustained disruption** of discussions, issues, or pull request reviews
- **Threats** of any kind — explicit or implied
- **Impersonation** of other contributors, maintainers, or community members
- Any other conduct that a reasonable person would consider inappropriate in a professional setting

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all project spaces, including:

- The GitHub repository (issues, pull requests, discussions, code reviews, commit messages)
- Any official communication channels associated with the project
- Public representation of the project (e.g., social media, conferences, events)

It also applies when an individual is officially representing the project in public contexts.

## Reporting Violations

If you experience or witness behavior that violates this Code of Conduct, please report it as soon as possible.

**How to report:**

- **Email:** conduct@percivo.app *(placeholder — update before public release)*
- **GitHub:** If comfortable, you may open a private issue or contact a maintainer directly via GitHub

When reporting, please include:

- A description of the incident
- Any relevant links, screenshots, or evidence
- Your contact information (optional, but helpful for follow-up)

All reports will be handled with confidentiality and care. The identity of the reporter will not be disclosed without explicit consent.

## Enforcement

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing this Code of Conduct. They will review all reports promptly and respond in a manner they determine to be appropriate given the circumstances.

### Enforcement Tiers

| Severity | Response |
|----------|---------|
| **Minor** — first-time, unintentional, or low-impact violation | Private written warning with explanation of the issue |
| **Moderate** — repeated, targeted, or deliberately harmful behavior | Temporary suspension from project participation |
| **Severe** — sustained harassment, threats, doxxing, or egregious conduct | Permanent ban from all project spaces |

Maintainers reserve the right to remove, edit, or reject any contributions, comments, issues, or other content that do not align with this Code of Conduct.

## Maintainer Responsibilities

Project maintainers commit to:

- Modeling the behavior expected of all contributors
- Reviewing reports fairly, confidentially, and in a timely manner
- Taking appropriate and consistent action in response to violations
- Not retaliating against anyone who reports in good faith
- Revisiting and improving this Code of Conduct as the community evolves

Maintainers who fail to uphold or enforce the Code of Conduct may be removed from their role by other maintainers.
